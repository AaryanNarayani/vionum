## Typography

**Sans:** Inter (400, 500, 600, 700)  
**Mono:** JetBrains Mono (400, 500)  
**Display:** Inter (700, 800) — no separate display font, use heavier weights

### Usage

- Headlines: `font-sans font-bold` or `font-extrabold`
- Body: `font-sans font-normal`
- Numbers, timestamps, addresses: `font-mono tabular-nums`
- Code: `font-mono`

## Tone & Voice

**Terse, precise, trustworthy.** Vionum is infrastructure — it doesn't hype, it delivers. Copy is active voice, specific, and compressed. "Obvious outcomes settled in minutes" not "We help you settle outcomes faster."

**No buzzwords.** No "revolutionizing," no "game-changing," no "next-generation." Say what it does.

**Trust through transparency.** Explain how it works, show the evidence trail, acknowledge the fallback. Users are underwriting settlement risk — respect that.

## Dos and Don'ts

### Color

✅ Use `--primary` for verified states, confidence indicators, success  
✅ Use neutral grays for most UI chrome  
✅ Reserve accent colors for meaningful state (resolved, escalated)  
❌ Don't use bright colors for decoration  
❌ Don't mix warm and cool grays

### Typography

✅ Use tabular nums for all changing numbers  
✅ Keep line-height tight on headlines (1.1–1.2)  
✅ Use mono for technical data (timestamps, hashes, precision)  
❌ Don't use display fonts — Inter bold is enough  
❌ Don't go below 14px for body text

### Copy

✅ "Resolved in 2m 14s" — specific, compressed  
✅ "Confidence: 96%" — data-forward  
✅ "Escalated to human review" — transparent  
❌ "Amazing speed!" — hype  
❌ "Click here to learn more" — vague  
❌ "Revolutionary AI technology" — buzzword soup
